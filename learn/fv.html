Fact Verification

Fact Verification (FV) is a subtask of Fact Extraction and VERification (FEVER) and can be formulated as whether the relationship of given claim and evidence is refute, support, or not enough information [Du et al., 2021] [Thorne et al., 2018].

Task

FV is a classification task that is a subtask of the Fact Extraction and Verification task. Fact Extraction and Verification consists of automatically extracting and verifying facts from textual sources. The FV subtask aims to infer whether the relationship between two sentences is that the first attacks or supports the second, or whether there is not enough information to refute or support the second sentence.

Since it is a classification task, the model uses the output corresponding to the [CLS] to make the prediction. The prediction is done with a Feed Forward neural network and a softmax layer, giving the confidence of each possible classification label.

FEVER Dataset

The Fever Dataset was introduced in FEVER: a large-scale dataset for Fact Extraction and VERification and consists of 185,445 claims generated by altering sentences extracted from Wikipedia and subsequently verified without knowledge of the sentence they were derived from.
